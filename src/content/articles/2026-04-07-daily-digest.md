## A Listeria-Laden Day: Recalls on the Rise

Did you know that in 2026 alone, we've already seen 286 food recalls? That's an average of about 13 recalls every week, making your grocery shopping experience feel more like a game of Russian roulette than a straightforward errand. 

Today, we dive into the most alarming recalls, focusing particularly on those that can put your health at risk.

## Class I Recalls: The Serious Stuff

### Made Fresh Salads Inc. - Cream Cheese Recalled

- **Products**: 
  - Whipped Cream Cheese, 5 lb white plastic tub
  - Lox Cream Cheese, 5 lb white plastic tub
  - Scallion Cream Cheese, 5 lb white plastic tub
  - Strawberry Cream Cheese, 5 lb white plastic tub
- **Reason**: Potential contamination with *Listeria monocytogenes*
- **Recall Dates**: April 2026 (specific date not provided)

Listeria is no joke; it can cause severe illness, particularly in vulnerable populations like pregnant women, newborns, the elderly, and those with weakened immune systems. The FDA has classified these as Class I recalls, which means they pose a serious risk of health consequences.

### House Of Flavors, Inc. - Ice Cream Recall

- **Product**: House of Flavors 4893 Chicago Vanilla Naturally Flavored Ice Cream, 3 GAL tub
- **Reason**: Undeclared egg
- **Recall Status**: Not Yet Classified

While undeclared allergens can cause allergic reactions that range from mild to life-threatening, it's worth noting that this recall hasn't yet received a classification. So, if you're a fan of this ice cream, it might be wise to avoid it for now.

## Class II Recalls: A Little Less Urgent, But Still Concerning

### Uncle Ray's LLC - Potato Chips

- **Product**: Lowes Foods sour cream and onion flavored potato chips, 8oz. bag
- **Reason**: Inaccurate nutritional data, specifically understated sodium content
- **Recall Date**: April 2026 (specific date not provided)

Sodium is a sneaky culprit in many snacks, and consumers are increasingly aware of its health implications. If you're watching your sodium intake, this recall could affect you more than you think.

### Willamette Valley Pie Company, LLC - Blueberry Crumble Pies

- **Products**: 
  - Frozen Raw Bulk 9 inch Blueberry Crumble Pie
  - Frozen Raw Bulk 8 inch Blueberry Crumble Pie
- **Reason**: Potential contamination with *Listeria monocytogenes*
- **Recall Dates**: April 2026 (specific dates not provided)

Two separate recalls for potential listeria contamination from the same company? It seems blueberries aren't the only thing getting a sour note in these pies.

### CNC Noodle Corporation - Undeclared Wheat

- **Product**: STEAM NOODLES
- **Reason**: Undeclared wheat
- **Recall Date**: April 2026 (specific date not provided)

Undeclared allergens like wheat can be dangerous for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease. It's crucial to read labels carefully, as this recall highlights how easily such oversights can happen.

### Cocos Wholesale and Imports, LLC - Cannoli Making Kit

- **Product**: Coco's Italian Market Cannoli Making Kit
- **Reason**: Misleading ingredient labeling regarding flour and ricotta cheese
- **Recall Date**: April 2026 (specific date not provided)

A cannoli kit should evoke thoughts of delicious desserts, not potential health hazards. Always check your ingredients, even when indulging your sweet tooth.

## Verdict

With 286 recalls so far this year, today's updates serve as a stark reminder that food safety is more critical than ever. Whether it's listeria lurking in your cream cheese or undeclared allergens in your snacks, staying informed is your best defense against foodborne illness. 

Inspector Morsel says: "If you thought grocery shopping was just picking up a few snacks, think again. It's a scavenger hunt for safety in a world filled with hidden hazards. Stay alert, stay informed, and for the love of all things edible, read those labels!"
