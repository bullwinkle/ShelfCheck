# ShelfCheck Business Plan

> AI-powered food safety accountability — holding brands to account with data, not opinions.

## Executive Summary

ShelfCheck is a data-driven food safety accountability site that collects FDA recall data twice daily, scores brands on their safety track record, and publishes AI-generated articles through an editorial mascot (Inspector Morsel 🦝). The site is live at https://bullwinkle.js.org/ShelfCheck/ with 580 recalls tracked, 258 brands scored, and daily digest articles generating automatically.

**The opportunity:** Food safety information is fragmented, hard to search, and buried in government databases. ShelfCheck makes it accessible, searchable, and actionable — giving consumers the power to make informed choices. There's no consumer-facing "brand safety score" product in this space.

**The goal:** Grow from a technical MVP to a revenue-generating content platform. Target: $3,000-6,000/mo within 12 months through newsletter subscriptions, affiliate revenue, API access, and sponsorships.

## Risk Analysis

### Legal Risks
- **Defamation claims from brands** — Mitigated: all data is from public FDA records. Scores are mathematical, not editorial opinion. Add clear methodology page and "Data sourced from openFDA" disclaimers.
- **FDA data accuracy** — We report what FDA publishes. Add disclaimer: "Data sourced from openFDA. Verify with manufacturer for latest status."

### Data Quality Risks
- **FDA API downtime** — `continue-on-error: true` in workflows handles this. Add monitoring.
- **Stale data** — 180-day collection window is sufficient. Consider extending to 1 year.
- **Brand name normalization** — Different spellings of same company may create duplicate brands. Add alias mapping.

### Competition Risks
- **FDA's own site** — Terrible UX, no scores, no analysis. Not a real competitor.
- **FoodSafety.gov** — Government site, informational only. No brand accountability.
- **Private recall alert apps** — Exist but don't score brands. ShelfCheck's angle is accountability, not just alerts.
- **Someone with more resources copies the idea** — First-mover advantage + SEO authority + Inspector Morsel brand. Move fast.

### Technical Risks
- **GitHub Actions limits** — Free tier: 2,000 min/month. Current usage is well under. Monitor.
- **GitHub Models API changes** — Have fallback prompt templates. Consider local model fallback.
- **GitHub Pages limits** — 1GB storage, 100GB bandwidth/month. Sufficient for years.

### Mitigation Priority
1. Add legal disclaimers (Week 1)
2. Set up Actions failure notifications (Week 1)
3. Add brand name alias mapping (Week 2)
4. Monitor API quotas monthly
